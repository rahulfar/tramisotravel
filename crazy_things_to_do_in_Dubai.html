10 crazy things to do in Dubai

When you’re in Dubai, it's not good to keep calm. Experience ultimate fun, superb adventure, and engage in some thrilling activities that drive you mad. Here, we bring to you 10 crazy things to do in Dubai :

1. Swim with Sharks at the Dubai Aquarium –

Test your courage by entering the deep waters and encountering the savage fish. The Dubai Aquarium has a 10 million liter tank that houses over 33,000 aquatic animals that include shark, stingrays, and the spectacular reef fish. No matter if you’re experienced or not, you'll get to taste thrilled at the Dubai Aquarium. The shark dive for the certified open water enthusiasts would give them a chance to face sand tigers, zebra, and nurse sharks among others. The naïve underwater lovers, without a diving license, can still have a glance at the magnanimous fish from behind the bars. But the best thing to go for is the Shark Encounter, wherein you get to experience the violent feeding frenzy.
Entrance Fee: From AED620 (~S$233) for Shark Walker
Opening Hours: 10 AM – 2 AM (Experience timings vary; 30-minute duration each)
How to get there: Take the Metro, or public buses 27 (from Deira Gold Souk station) or 29 (from Al Ghubaiba bus station) to the Dubai Mall. Dubai Aquarium is located on the ground level

2.Go Skydiving at Palm Jumeirah -

Dubai is one of the best places to go for an exciting and thrilling sport like skydiving. At Skydive Dubai, you get a chance to free fall over the vast stretch of desert or over the most beautiful of all Palm Jumeirah Island, which is an epic manmade Island.  If it’s your first diving experience, then you’re sure to fall in love with the sport after this. And the experienced ones too throng to this place to taste the skies and witness the enthralling Dubai skyline. A separate skydiver captures your entire jump, so don’t forget to pose nicely during your free fall. Skydiving is one of the most sought after activities in Abu Dhabi, so one should book at least one month in advance.
Cost: AED2199 (~S$826)
Opening Hours: 10 AM to Sunset (Timings vary)
How to get there: Take the Red Line to Damac Properties, followed by the T1 tram to Marina Towers, and walk ~1.7km

3.Go Sand Dune Bashing-

Dubai desert safari has a lot to offer to its visitors but for the thrill-seekers, there can’t be anything better than sand dune bashing. Once you enter the desert region for this exciting sport, air would be let out of your car tires to make the car suitable to run wild on the sand mounds. Then for the next half hour, you’ll be riding on the rough desert surface intermittently coming across steeps and mounds. This is some kind of a roller coaster only that would give you a body shake. If you wish to be the rider, you can even place yourself in the driver’s seat in the dune buggy and head with great vigor towards the vast sands.
Cost: ~S$43.90 per person including roundtrip pick-up, camel ride, and dinner via Klook
Pick-up Times: Between 2:30 PM – 3:15 PM (timing to be confirmed with driver)

4. Spend a night in the world’s first seven-star hotel-

The magnificent Burj Al Arab is in reality a five-star hotel but due to its enchanting resplendence it's called a seven- star hotel. This iconic sail-shaped hotel is indeed costly but then it’s worth a visit for a once in a lifetime experience. You’ll be staying in a suite with your own personal butler. The majestic furniture, extravagant lobby, the first-class service, everything about this place is just mesmerizing. The classic hotel is bathed in gold as over two tons of real gold embedded in furniture, taps, shower-heads, and even the dustbin. The Burj Al Arab has also played host to some high-profile events, including a tennis match between Roger Federer and Andre Agassi.
Cost: From ~S$1,990 per night
Opening Hours: Check-in from 3 PM
How to get there: Take the Red Line to Mall of Emirates Station. Then, take bus 81 for eight stops, alighting at Wild Wadi 2 bus stop.

5. Ride a hot air balloon-

Well, why not experience the city from all angles. After riding on the sands, diving deep under the waters it's a great plan to soar high. Have a clear glimpse of the vibrant colors at the horizon, which is indeed a perfect treat to the eye. A hot air balloon ride gives you the birds-eye view of the vast stretch of desert. If you are lucky enough you can spot the magnificent gazelles dancing across the sands. Watch the peregrine falcon, the national symbol of UAE, and the world's fastest animal perform for you. Complimentary to this ride you get breakfast at a majestic Bedouin camp. So, there cannot be a better start of the day at Abu Dhabi than this.
Cost: ~S$419.45 via Klook (includes roundtrip hotel pick-up and breakfast)
Pick-up Times: From 4 AM – 5 AM (timing to be confirmed with driver)

6. Go Skiing-

-Dubai is the place where the north meets the south. With temperatures soaring in the vast desert region, at the Mall of the Emirates, you’ll see chilling winter prevailing. One ought to try out skiing in the world’s biggest indoor ski resort. If you haven’t tried the sport ever before, take the initiative here. You’re sure to fall in love with the alpine activities like bobsleds, penguin encounters, and zorb balls. With the professional instructors who are really patient and friendly, you would have fun learning and practicing skiing. However, if you're already good at your snowboards, waste no time in hitting the indoor slopes. The atmosphere is really chilly with temperature reaching -2 degree Celcius. Although winter clothing is provided, the temperatures are really low, and you could bring extra layers to enjoy at this winter wonderland.
Entrance Fee: ~S$111.09 for the Polar Express Pass (includes full-day snow park access, chairlift ride, winter clothing, and a Ski Discovery Lesson or access to the slopes)
Opening Hours: 10AM – 11PM (Sun – Wed); 10AM – 12 AM (Thu); 9AM – 12AM (Fri); 9AM – 11PM (Sat)
How to get there: Take the Red Line to Mall of Emirates Station, then head to the ground floor

7. Take a snapshot at the world’s tallest building-

Dubai is known for its magnificent skyscrapers, but the one that is Dubai’s pride is Burj Khalifa, the world’s tallest building. This 828 m high architectural marvel houses 160 floors. It is not just the outside view that is breathtaking, even the interiors are a sight to behold. The visual display in the elevators takes you back to the journey of making the Burj. Get the experience of visiting the world's highest observation deck at level 148. There are plenty of options for you to capture this majestic building on the camera. You can shoot from the top, below, have a close-up or click from afar, every shot would just be simply enthralling. It'll be a great idea to purchase the tickets well in advance since it has twin benefits. Not only would this save you from waiting in long queues but You’ll also get to avail of some fabulous discounts.
Entrance Fee: From ~S$50
Opening Hours: 8:30 AM – 12 AM (last entry at 11:15 PM)
How to get there: Take the Dubai Metro Red Line to Dubai Mall Station. From there, walk through the Link Bridge for about 15 minutes to the Dubai Mall. Follow the mall’s signage to the Lower Ground floor, towards the Burj Khalifa.

8. Try camel milk-

Give yourself a healthy treat in Dubai by trying camel milk. The milk of a camel is rich in vitamin C and iron and will help you get rid of the weariness from the travel. Camel's milk isn’t strong as compared to that of other animals like goat and has got a watery texture. You can enjoy this light drink at various places in Dubai. You can try a glass at the Al Majlis cafe in Dubai Mall (AED18.90, ~S$7.11) or at the quaint Arabian Tea House (AED17, ~S$6) in Al Fahidi Historical Neighbourhood.
Cost: From AED17 (~S$6)
Opening Hours: 10 AM – 11 PM (Al Majlis); 7 AM – 10 PM (Arabian Tea House)
How to get there: From the Grand Atrium on the ground floor of the Dubai Mall, turn left and walk past Cartier and Bvlgari towards the Souk Atrium (Al Majlis); Take the Green Line to Al Fahidi Station, then walk 9 mins (Arabian Tea House)

9. Cross the Creek on an Abra-

The traditional abras are an indispensable part of Arabian culture and it’s always a delight to take an Abra ride across the picturesque Dubai creek. Admire the magnificent Dubai skyline as you cross the waters on an Abra with your beloved partner. That would make for a perfect romantic setting. Dubai creek connects Bur Dubai and Deira Dubai, both of which are great places to shop. At Bur Dubai, you’ll find a vibrant textile market selling pashmina scarves, woven bags, lamps, and accessories. In Deira Dubai, you’ll find peddlers selling spice, gold, and perfume. So, if you want to see these amazing old markets in Dubai take an Abra which would cost you only 1AED (~S$0.38).
Cost: 1AED (~S$0.38)
Opening Hours: 5 AM – 12 AM
How to get there: Take the metro to Al Fahidi Station, then walk ten minutes to Bur Dubai. Along the creek, you’ll find many abras which can take you across to the other side.

10. Ride the world’s fastest Roller coaster, Formula Rossa-

The craziest thing to do in Dubai is to ride on the world's fastest roller coaster, Formula Rossa in Ferrari World. It requires guts to step up for this savage ride. Buckle up, secure your safety goggles, and be firmly seated against the backrest as it’s no joke to speed up from 0 to 240 km/h in 4.9 seconds. But undoubtedly, it is super fun and the euphoria would last for a long time.
Entrance Fee: From ~S$95.35
Opening Hours: 11 AM – 8 PM (varies by season so do check online before visiting)
How to get there: Located a few hours away from Dubai, get to Ferrari World in Abu Dhabi via Bus 190 from Dubai’s Al Ghubaiba Bus Station
