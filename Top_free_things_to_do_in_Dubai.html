Top 12 free things to do in Dubai

Dubai is known for its resplendence. The magnificent 5-star hotels, brilliant skyscrapers, luxurious malls are what Dubai is known for, all of which seem quite expensive. But there’s a lot that you can enjoy for free too. Can't believe it. Check it out then.

1. Visit Aquaventure Waterpark-

A visit to the Acquaventure Waterpark at Atlantis, The Palm Hotel is definitely a costly deal throughout the year except for one day and that’s your birthday. All you need to do is enter your birthday on their website to avail of the “ free Acquaventure Waterpark birthday offer” voucher. But remember this is valid only on your birthday and the following six days. So the next time plan your visit to Dubai on your birthday and get the birthday splash in one of the most thrilling water parks of Dubai and that too absolutely free!

2.Free walking tour of Dubai:

It’s not a bad idea to walk around the city. You can walk alone or with your travel mates. Not only is it free but provides a great opportunity to closely explore the city. Maybe you come across some unexplored place which is all the more exciting and magnificent! You can also associate with a company like Airo Tour which offers free walking tours around the city for between 2 and 20 people at a time. You won’t pay for the tour but AiroTour asks that you tip your guide if you are impressed by his job.

3.Take a tour of Bur Dubai Grand Mosque-

-Besides heading to the Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque, which is indeed a magnificent one, this time you could visit Bur Dubai instead. You can get a free tour of the Grand Mosque in one of Dubai’s oldest and most authentic neighborhoods. The guides at the Grand Mosque are associated with the Islamic Information Center so they know everything there is to know about the mosque and Islam. A visit to this mosque would indeed be an enriching experience that would provide great solace and tranquility to your soul. The mosque is close to the textile souk and the Dubai Museum so there’s plenty around to explore and enjoy. Open Sunday through Thursday 9 – 11.15 am. Near the Ruler’s Court Office, Ali Bin Abi Taleb St, Bur Dubai

4. Go cycling in the desert-

Cycling at Al Qudra’s 50km of track is one of the best free things to do in Dubai. You can easily get a road bike and helmet on rent from Trek Dubai at a very minimal cost. But the experience of cycling on the red Dubai sands is extraordinary. Enjoy the sunset as you move along on the vast stretch of sand. The sight of an odd camel and even an oryx is stunning. Best of all? It’s free.

5. See the Ras Al Khor flamingos--

You can see hundreds of pink flamingos that live in the water near the junction of Ras Al Khor Road and the Dubai-Al Ain highway, but how would it be to have a closer look at these magnificent birds! Grab a pair of binoculars at the Ras Al Khor wildlife sanctuary and hide out in one of the three wooden birding hides near the water’s edge. The mesmerizing beauty of these birds is enthralling and you can surely capture them in your camera. The entrance is free and the huts are open between 9 am and 4 pm Saturday through Thursday. It’s a wonderful free activity to do with children.

6. Hit the beach-

Dubai has some great free, public beaches where you won’t have to spend a dirham. But these beaches are indeed one of the most exciting places to visit in Abu Dhabi. You can even camp at some beaches and engage in absolute fun with your tour mates. JBR Beach, Kite Beach Dubai, and Umm Suqeim Park Beach are three of the most popular. JBR Beach has a lot of attractions for kids and you can spend as much or as little as you want in the nearby cafes, shops, and restaurants.

7. Go camping-

There cannot be anything better than camping in the most astonishing outdoors in Dubai. There are plenty of spots to camp in the desert just a few kilometers out of the city. Not just the deserts but can also camp on hills, at the beach, or go for the classic forest camping. The most suitable places for camping are Fujairah beach, Liwa, Acacia forests, Al Qudra Lakes, Umm Al Quwain beach, and Wadi Sana. With lots of ex- pats leaving in the summer months you can often pick up some great deals on pre-loved camping equipment.

8. Check out the art at AlSerkal Avenue-

-Give a treat to your eyes and soul by admiring some wonderful artwork at AlSerkal Avenue. Get a deep insight into the eclectic diverse Arabian culture and learn from the land's history. The Al Quoz gallery district is free to enter and you can wander in and out of as many galleries, pop-up shops, or concept spaces as you like, and finally stop for a hot drink or cold-pressed juice at one of the coffee shops if you fancy some.

9. Visit Coffee Museum Dubai -

This is a sort of museum-come-shop-come-shrine for all coffee lovers. Located in a villa in the Al Fahidi neighborhood, the Coffee Museum Dubai takes you on a tour of the history of coffee. If you wish to know about the origin and journey of this magical bean that sits at your table every morning and refreshes you, then this is the right place for you. It has an impressive collection of coffee-related artifacts, including a 300-year-old coffee pot. There are books about coffee, old examples of coffee advertising, and the whole museum has got a lovely aroma because of the roasting demonstrations. The staff is really knowledgeable and they can answer any and all of your coffee-related questions. Entry to this unique museum is free but you would be tempted to buy a cup of coffee and it is definitely worth it.

10. Free yoga classes-

If you are a yoga enthusiast, Dubai has great options for you to practice this traditional art. Fitness freaks would find it a boon to enjoy free yoga lessons all the way in Abu Dhabi. “Friends of yoga” organizes daily yoga classes at 5 different locations in Dubai which are: Deira Creek, BurDubai Creek, Zabeel Park, JLT Park, and Internet City. There are both morning and evening sessions and you can plan as per your itinerary. So you can keep your fitness routine while on your Dubai trip and that too absolutely free!

11. Free Entry To The Jumeirah Beach Corniche-

The Jumeirah Beach Corniche has recently become a free attraction in Dubai. A visit to this place is a true delight as it offers so much to all its visitors and that too absolutely free. It offers 800 meters of beachfront, jogging track, showers, children’s play areas, and shaded picnic areas. The 14 km long corniche runs from Dubai Marine Beach Resort down to Burj Al Arab Hotel. Plan a visit here to have a great family time for which you need not spend a penny. The place is open from 7:30 am to 10:00 pm.

12. Free Dance to the peppy numbers-

Do you know, you can even enjoy Dubai’s nightlife for free. You may not believe it, but Societe Dubai has it for you. Visit this newly opened nightclub on a Friday night and dance the night out as the DJ plays the best hits from the '80s and '90s. The ambiance too is just perfect. Shabby-chic environment, leather sofas, and oversized lampshades are what defines this classic nightclub. Situated in the Marina Byblos Hotel, Dubai Marina, Societe Dubai is a must-visit for all party lovers. And the best thing – Entry here is absolutely free!
