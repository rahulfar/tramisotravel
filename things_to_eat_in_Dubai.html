8 things to eat in Dubai

Dubai is one of the best countries to visit and can offer you some of the best experiences to have in your life, but there is one more thing which Dubai offers you. You can have some of the best dishes to have in Dubai, following are some of the best things to eat when you visit Dubai:-

1. Baklava –

Baklava is a very sweet and rich dessert pastry made from many layers of filling filled with small chopped nuts and sweetened and made together with syrup, frosting and possibly honey too. Baklava has the characteristics of the cuisines of the Levant and the broader Middle East, along with the South Caucasus, Balkans, the Maghreb and Central Asia. Baklava is sweet and tasty dessert. It is used to be served in high end Restaurants. Its pricing is approximately AED 39.50 in Dubai. Baklava is a must try in dessert in Dubai. It’s a Greek favorite which makes everyone happy because of its sweet and good taste. It is one of the most loved dessert in Dubai by both the tourists and permanent residents.

2. Turkish Ice Cream -

Dondurma is one of the best Turkish mastic ice cream, which is very tasty. Turkish ice cream is similar to the Syrian dessert. Dondurma ice cream includes the ingredients cream, salep mastic, whipped cream and sugar. The ice cream is believed to be originated from the city and region of Maraş. Turkish ice cream are so good that they are all sweet, creamy, stretchy and sticky at the same point of time. The ice cream is stretchy stickiness because of the inclusion of an aromatic resin which is known as mastic. Turkish ice cream is a world famous dessert which is available in many countries. It is usually considered as a street food as the ice is sold in stalls. Its pricing is average and it is not expensive. The seller also play with you while giving you the ice cream as they have a special stick in which the ice cream remains stick and they can play with it. It is a must try dessert.

3. Manousheh – Pizza of Dubai -

Manousheh is a Lebanese breakfast item which is typically eaten on the go and it is found in corner bakeries all over the country of Dubai. The Manousheh pizza is considered as the Arabic pizza, and it is originated from the Lebanon, the Manousheh can be rolled or be open like a pizza and it is filled with zatar which is some type of arabic herbes mix with cheese, and with many other ingredients. The Manousheh pizza is a Lebanese breakfast item which is typically eaten on the go and found in corner bakeries of the country. The dough is baked in house and the dough has a crispy exterior and warm chewy interior with some vegetarian or meat toppings. It is a delicious and special type of pizza only found in the bakeries of Dubai.

4. Tabbouleh – Salad veg -

Tabbouleh is a type of Levantine vegetarian salad which is made mostly from finely chopped parsley, tomatoes, bulgur, seasoned, onion and mint with olive oil, salt and sweet pepper and lemon juice. Some people also add lettuce, or use semolina instead of bulgur in tabbouleh. Tabbouleh is traditionally served as a part of mezze in many of the Arab countries. The salad is very commonly found in Dubai and many residents finds it very delicious. It can be ordered in any restaurant of Dubai and its pricing is also good its not that expensive. Tabbouleh can be termed as most liked salad in Dubai as well as in most of the arab countries.

5. Kellaj – Cheesy -

Kellaj is a very special and celebrated dish in many Arab countries and specially in Dubai. Kellaj is one of the most top rated dishes of all time in Dubai, Kellaj is also consistently as the top pick most of the times. Kellaj is basically a charcoal grilled bread which is filled with a lots of Haloumi cheese. Kellaj is an ideal snacking in most of the arab countries and it also fills up the stomach of the people. Kellaj is a must try snack in Dubai. You can pick any flight you want to and visit the best restaurants in Dubai and get ready to fill your stomach with some amazing and tasty snacks. In some premium restaurants its price is above average but the experience and taste is worth it.

6. Shish Tawook -

Shish tawook is a popular non veg dish of Dubai, which is very juicy and tasty. Shish tawook is traditional made with marinated chicken shish kebab of Ottoman cuisine that later became part of Middle Eastern cuisine also and now it is apart of middle eastern countries. Shish tawook is widely eaten in countries like Armenia, Turkey, Azerbaijan, Lebanon, Egypt, Jordan, Syria, Palestine, Iraq, and Israel. There is also a similar dish in Persian cuisine which is the traditional jujeh kabab, both are very similar to each other. The dish is served in kebab houses in many cities around the world but specially in Dubai. The dish is made from cubes of chicken that are marinated, then they are grilled till Shish tawook reaches its beautiful color. Shish tawook is commonly marinades based on the yogurt and lemon juice or tomato puree, though there are other variations of the dish and all are good enough to spend the money. The dish can be consumed either in sandwich form or on a plate with some vegetables and sometimes with rice or French fries also.

7. Khanfaroosh – Teatime Dessert -

Khanfaroosh is a traditional is of Arab countries and it a tea time dessert in many Arab countries and specially in Dubai. People love to have this Tea time dessert and it is one of the most popular desert in the country of Dubai. Khanfaroosh is traditional a fried sweet famous in Dubai and surrounding regions of the Gulf countries. It is made from rice flour, eggs, rose water, baking powder and cardamon saffron. Khanfarooshs are fried and eaten warm, which gives them their rich flavour. The dish is extremely popular during Ramadan and visiting ceremonies in the country. Khanfaroosh is a heritage and Traditional Food of Dubai from many years. Khanfaroosh is affordable too and is very commonly eaten by the people in the country.

8. Taboon Bread -

Taboon bread and also know as laffa is a Levantine flatbread which is very popular in all the Arab countries. The bread is traditionally baked in a taboon oven or a tannur on medium to high flame, and it is very similar to the various tandoor breads found in many parts of Asia, specially in India. The bread is used as a base or wrap in many dishes and eaten in different ways. The taboon bread is of medium thickness and slightly chewy which does not tear easily. Taboon bread is traditionally baked on a bed of small hot stones in the taboon oven and it is a important part of the Arabic culture. The bread is the base of musakhan and it is often considered as the national dish of Palestine. In Palestine the folded flat- bread is often filled with a spinach and onion mixture, or with cheese curds to make it more delicious. The ordinary taboon-breads are slightly smaller in size than the ordinary tannur-bread which is also very common in some countries. So, taboon is a dish to have whenever you visit an Arabic country and it is very affordable to have.
