7 Unexplored places of the world

All of us are thrilled when we hear of an adventurous trip with friends. Aren't we? But this seems a distant reality amidst the situation of the pandemic that the world is going through. It's risky to move out of our homes, but what's the harm in exploring the Unexplored from our homes. Here are the seven unexplored places of the world that would thrill and excite you.

1. Greenland Icecaps â€“

Inhabited by just 56,000 people, Greenland is the home to glaciers, ice flowing rivers, midnight sun, hot springs and northern lights. The sight of the humongous glaciers is sure to freeze you up.

2. Mariana trench, Western Pacific Ocean -

The deepest point being 10,000 metres, Mariana trench is a dangerous place to explore. Hiding in it are many secrets of Earth, but mind you it needs guts to touch the floor of the Earth.

3. Gangadhar Puensum, Bhutan -

Bhutan government restricted mountaineering in this highest unclimbed mountain of height 7,570 metres.The only four reported expeditions turned out unsuccessful and people attached spiritual beliefs to this place. So if you think you can conquer the world by climbing Mt. Everest, you're wrong. There is a mountain unclimbed and unexplored.

4. Deserts -

Extreme temperature fluctuations have made deserts like Sahara in Africa to be untouched by human hand and mind. Humans are not adaptable to the harsh climatic conditions there as some plants and animals are.

5. Deep caves -

The unbearable weather conditions, scalding water, sharp rocks and slipping conditions make caves to be the least explored places. The underwater, crystal and snow caves are even more dangerous for expedition. So, do you wonder some ascetics meditating in these caves or do you fathom some aliens residing there.

6. Amazon Rainforest, South America -

The rich biodiversity makes Amazon Rainforest a focal area for scientific expeditions. But it's mysterious habitat makes it still a least explored place on Earth. It's just those Discovery channel superheroes like Bear Grylls who venture out to this deadly place.

7. Antarctica -

With the lowest recorded temperature being -89 degree celcius, Antarctica is the driest, coldest and windiest continent on Earth. The fastest wind speed recorded in Antarctica is 200 miles per hour in 1972. The ice sheets in Antarctica have a thickness of 2 miles. Don't these facts freeze you up, but they make Antarctica a hazardous visiting place.
